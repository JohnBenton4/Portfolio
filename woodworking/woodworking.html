Woodworking

Please scroll through the images below to see samples of my work.

Vanity Bench

This bench was designed in collaboration with the client who wanted a piece that was both beautiful and functionally secure. Ash is a strong wood, so I chose it for the seat and legs. The lumber was very stable as well thanks to the straight grain. I chose to accentuate the chatoyancy in the ash with a walnut cross member and walnut pin for the mortise and tenon.

Walnut Fireplace Mantel

This mantel was designed to resemble a beam mantel, but I built it in a box style. The inside is hollow which allowed me to mount it in the clients home when there was limited support above the fireplace. This image shows the amazing grain in this walnut lumber. There were only a few areas where holes needed to be filled. I used black epoxy to fill the holes and it turned out great. Here you can see the bookmatched end caps I used on the mantel. This view also emphasizes the beautiful satin finish that goes so well with the black brick.

Coat Rack

This design is a conglomerate of new and antique. The age old top shelf and vintage hooks give this walnut coat rack an aged feel. While the dimensions and use of black epoxy show a more contemporary style. I used mortise and tenon techniques to ensure a secure fit and finished the piece off with one of my favorite homemade lacquers. The client will use this coat rack for generations.

Clock

"We must use time wisely and forever realize that the time is always ripe to do right," Nelson Mandela. I designed this clock with two ideas in mind. Time is rock solid. It doesn't move or bend. Time also allots us the opportunity to do good. I felt like ash was the perfect wood to represent to first thought, time is solid. Ash is a very hard and dense wood that is very rigid. It symbolizes times relentless consistency. I felt it was important for the finished product to be excellent. That way I was creating something "good" with the time I put into it.
